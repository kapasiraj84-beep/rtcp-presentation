Three Core Problems

Why Farmers Lose Money

1
Middlemen Exploitation & Market Cartels
APMC market agents form cartels, restricting competition and capturing large margins between MSP/auction prices and retail prices. Middlemen control price information and quote below prevailing rates. Many act as moneylenders, tying farmers to high-interest loans repaid by forcing sales at depressed prices.
Source: IDinsight, Jacobin 2024
2
Information Asymmetry & Price Manipulation
Farmers lack access to real-time market prices and depend on traders for price information. This enables middlemen to manipulate prices, especially during bumper crops or when farmers face pressure to sell quickly. Farmers' share of the final consumer rupee is often very low for fruits and vegetables.
Source: Earth.org, SmartU 2024
3
Storage Limitations & Forced Immediate Sales
Lack of storage facilities and perishability of crops force farmers to sell immediately after harvest when prices are at their lowest. Transport issues further increase reliance on middlemen. Small and marginal farmers (85% of total) are particularly vulnerable to price manipulation.
Source: Times of India, Jacobin 2024
